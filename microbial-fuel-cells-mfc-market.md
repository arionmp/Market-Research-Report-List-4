<p><p>Microbial Fuel Cells (MFC) are a type of bio-electrochemical systems that use microorganisms to generate electricity from organic waste. There are two main types of MFC markets: Mediator Microbial Fuel Cells, which utilize a mediator to facilitate electron transfer between the microorganisms and the electrodes, and Mediator-free Microbial Fuel Cells, which directly transfer electrons without the need for a mediator. Both types have their own advantages and applications in various industries such as wastewater treatment, renewable energy production, and biosensor development.</p></p>

<p><p>Gastro Esophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) drugs can be categorized into three main types: H2 receptor antagonists, Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs), and antacids. H2 receptor antagonists work by reducing the production of stomach acid, while PPIs block the enzyme that produces acid in the stomach. Antacids, on the other hand, neutralize stomach acid to provide quick relief from heartburn and acid reflux symptoms. Each type of drug targets different mechanisms in treating GERD, providing patients with various options for managing their symptoms.</p></p>

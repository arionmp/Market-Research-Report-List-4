<p><p>Retinal Vein Occlusion (RVO) Therapeutics market is divided into two main types: Branch Retinal Vein Occlusion (BRVO) and Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (CRVO). BRVO occurs when a small branch of the central retinal vein is blocked, leading to decreased blood flow in a specific area of the retina. On the other hand, CRVO occurs when the main central retinal vein is blocked, affecting a larger area of the retina. Different therapeutic approaches are used for each type of RVO to manage the condition and prevent vision loss.</p></p>

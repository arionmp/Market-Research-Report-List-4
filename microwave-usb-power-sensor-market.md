<p><p>Microwave USB Power Sensors are available in two main types: Silicon Photodiode and Germanium Photodiode. Silicon Photodiodes are less expensive and offer a wider spectral range, making them suitable for general purpose applications. On the other hand, Germanium Photodiodes are more sensitive and have a higher quantum efficiency in the near-infrared spectrum, making them ideal for precise measurements in specific applications such as telecommunications and spectroscopy. Both types of sensors are commonly used in various industries for measuring power levels in microwave frequencies through a USB connection.</p></p>

<p><p>Chained consumer food service markets typically involve two main types: commissary foodservice and serve foodservice. In commissary foodservice, food is prepared in a central kitchen or commissary and then distributed to various locations for final preparation and service. On the other hand, serve foodservice involves preparing and serving food on-site at each location. Both models have their own set of advantages and challenges, but ultimately aim to provide consumers with consistent and convenient dining experiences.</p></p>

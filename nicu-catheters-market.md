PICCs are inserted into a vein in the arm and threaded into a larger vein, CVCs are placed into a major vein in the chest, and UVCs are inserted into the umbilical vein in newborns. Other types include arterial catheters, endotracheal tubes, and feeding tubes, which are essential for neonatal care in the NICU.</p></p>

<p><p>DC Reactors are categorized into two main types: F-Class Coil DC Reactors and H-Class Coil DC Reactors. F-Class Coil DC Reactors are designed for general purpose applications where moderate levels of DC voltage are involved. On the other hand, H-Class Coil DC Reactors are specifically constructed to handle high levels of DC voltage and are typically used in high-power industrial applications. Both types of reactors play a crucial role in reducing harmonics and protecting electrical equipment from damage caused by sudden surges in the DC power supply.</p></p>
